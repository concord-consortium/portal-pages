Hello, GEODE Pilot Teachers!

This page gives you access to all the professional development and classroom support materials that you will need to successfully run the GEODE module with your students. Please start by clicking the button below to run the GEODE Teacher Edition, which adds a layer of teacher tips to the student version of the module.

By the end of the module, not only will you be familiar with the curriculum but also you will be an expert on teaching plate tectonics with the Seismic Explorer tool and Tectonic Explorer model.

The links below provide additional background information. We encourage you to read the Introduction to the GEODE Module before using the Teacher Edition to learn a little about why we structured the content in this way. Teaching with Online Materials provides helpful information about using computer-based curricula with your classes. Making Sense Across Representations provides an overview of the linkages between the maps, data displays, and models that are used in the GEODE module. The Plate Tectonics Learning Progression provides some insight into the student research that led to this GEODE module.

The Density Activity is provided as an optional resource. We have not specifically addressed the role of density in plate interactions within the module. We encourage you to teach your students about density, though, so that they can better understand why plates interact as they do. You may use the provided one-page Density activity, or you may use the resources that you've used in the past, or you can use a combination of the two.

Use the Teacher Dashboard Guide (watch How To Video) to learn how to track students' progress as they go through the GEODE module. The Summary Table is a good way to help students make connections between the different activities and to help them form a deeper understanding of the role of plate tectonics on Earth.
